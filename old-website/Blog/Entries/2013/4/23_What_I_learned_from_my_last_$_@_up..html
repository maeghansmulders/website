What I learned from my last #$*@ up.

Tuesday, 23 April, 2013

 


I’m young, I’m stupid and I make mistakes.


After walking something like 30 blocks.. talking to myself and waving my hands excessively in the air (kind of like Tom Cruise in Jerry McGuire)..... I find myself sitting at Notman House talking to my friend Zoe - she is always so supportive.  The conclusion of our talk was unintended, but this is what I learned from my last #$(@ up...


I’ll Never Do That Again!


I can’t even begin to describe how terrible I felt for 8 hours straight... seriously.. 8 hours..

Although my nerves stuck around longer then welcome... the lesson I learned was almost instant.  I can’t change what I’ve done, I can only do the best with what I have... and be honest moving forward.  


Crash fast, build faster and let your grief go.


I’m Glad I Put Myself Out There And Took A Risk. God Hates A Coward.


I tried something... and it didn’t work...... shit.


I’m not going say it’s easy to be the person standing on the edge preaching that you have the answer... especially when no one asked you a question...


But I am glad I made myself vulnerable and known as someone who cares.  I’ll continue to search for answers and share my ideas... no matter how stupid or naive they are. I am the girl who stood up and spoke... not the one who sat back and watched.


Cowards don’t steer change... it’s the people who are brave enough that do. 


I Think I Am Smarter Then That Now


It’s hard to tell sometimes... but experience is a great thing.


Being able to draw from past events and apply them to other situations makes me feel like I’m wiser than before.   I’m able to predict potential outcomes from what I’ve learned but also view obstacles from a different point of view.


Some of the most interesting people I know say they went through years of #$%@ up’s to get to where they are today.. 


So I guess in short, I’m a beginner.  I’m young, stupid and make mistakes..... but it’s ok - even the great start there too.



___



I don’t think my blog is life changing or NY Times worthy.. but it’s honest and from the heart.


thanks for reading.  


Just Maeghan
